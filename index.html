Matter-mations

An interactive and animated webpage to help you learn about the three states of matter.

Welcome! This webpage will help you learn about the three states of matter: solid, liquid, and gas. We will look at how the molecules in these three states behave and how their behaviour differs from the other states.

Along the way, you will be able to see the differences for yourself with three interactive animations.

Solid

A solid is quite possibly the simplest state of matter. The molecules in a solid are tightly compacted together, usually in some defined order or pattern. They form rigid objects that have a volume and shape that does not change as the molecules stay in their place, giving the object its solid shape and un-changeable volume. Of course, the molecules aren't completely frozen. They just don't move around enough to change anything about them like shape, volume, or rigidness. They sort of vibrate in one place.

Solid molecules are quite boring. They barely move at all! Take a look at this diagram below to see how the molecules move in a solid object.



Click and drag the mouse to create a shape of your choice and see how the solid molecules of your shape move around.



Liquid

Liquids are essentially melted solids. When a solid melts, it becomes a liquid. For example, ice, a solid, becomes water, a liquid, when it is melted. The molecules in a liquid are spaced out enough so that they can move around and can go over and under each other. They also move around faster than the slight vibration of solid molecules. Liquids have no definite shape but still have a definite volume. This allows liquids to be poured into different containers. In fact, liquids will take the shape of whatever container they have been poured into, even though their volume remains the same.

Liquid molecules are a bit more interesting than solids. They move around more which gives them the ability to flow. They are also more spaced out than solids. Take a look at this diagram below to see how liquid molecules move.



Click on the canvas 20 times and see how liquid molecules move around. Compare that to the movement and spacing of solid molecules.



Gas

Gas molecules, unlike solids or liquids, have neither a definite shape nor definite volume. This means that they are free-spirited and do not take the shape of any container. Gas molecules move much faster than solid or liquid molecules and are far more spaced out, allowing them to swirl and float around.

Gas molecules are great fun! They move around so quickly and they are spaced out so that they can swirl and flow over each other in beautiful motions. They are also much more spaced out than liquid molecules. Take a look at this diagram below to see how gas molecules move around.



Click on the canvas 20 times to see the speed, movement, and spacing of gas molecules. Compare that to the solid and liquid animations.
